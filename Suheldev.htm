From the 7th to the 10th century the Arabs, continuously invaded India through the North-West region. But, they achieved no concrete success and failed to poise themselves in India owing to the strong resistance and retaliatory blows by the Northern kingdoms. Gradually, they got weakened due to the repeated foreign invasions. The Hindu rulers of present-day Sindh and Aghanistan fell to Islamic rule after a bloody,relentless struggle of 200 years. The first Turkish invasion under Ghaznavi took place in 1001 and continued till 1034. The second invasion under Muhammed Ghori happened in 1191. But wait!wait!wait!!! Didn,t you notice a huge gap between these two Turkish invasions. Whereas, all the other previous invasions for the past 300 years happened in quick succession. What could have been the reason for this? Let's see:

Backdrop

The exploits of Mahmud Ghaznavi

In 1001 A.D.,The Turkish invader, Mahmud Ghaznavi, got the first breakthrough in Northern India by defeating the Shahis of Afghanistan and Punjab. From 1001 A.D. to 1025 A.D.,he continuously invaded the northern India 17 times,ravaging Punjab,Kashmir,Gwalior and Kannauj, obtaining immense fortunes from these regions.He mercilessly sacked the holy city of Mathura desecrating all the temples he came across and burning down the land of Lord Krishna.But,the greatest damage inflicted by him was the destruction of the Somnath temple,one of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrine of Lord Shiva. About 50,000 faithful patriots sacrificed their lives in an attempt to safeguard the sacred temple from the marauding Turks.

The invasion of Salar Masud

The wicked legacy of Ghaznavi was carried on by his nephew, Salar Masud. Masud differed from Ghaznavi both in aim and strategy.Ghaznavi invaded India with the chief aim of raiding,plundering and looting her riches. He didn't stay in India for long whenever he invaded. But, Masud wanted to convert the whole Indian subcontinent into Dar-al Islam (Islamic state) and wanted to establish permanent rule of Islam over India. Incited by religious fervour, he invaded India with a formidable force of 100,000. After conquering Punjab, he made successful incursions in Delhi, Rajasthan, Kannauj and Meerut. At some places, the kings accepted his suzerainty and embraced Islam while those who refused to accept his terms were subdued and the unarmed,innocent civilians became the victims of his bigotry through forced religious conversions and inhuman slaughter of those who were unwilling to accept their religion. Owing to the aforesaid circumstances, India would surely have become an Islamic nation and our future would have been completely different, if this vicious savagery would not have been halted by Maharaja Suheldev at the right time.

Suheldev

Suheldev was the king of Shravasti,near Bahraich. Masud who was triumphant in North India now eyed on the kingdom of Bahraich. He stationed himself near the present-day Lucknow and despatched his forces to suppress the nearby kingdoms. They got encountered with Suheldev's father's army who was at the opposite bank of the Bhakla river ready for a fierce battle in daylight. But, alas his adversaries followed no rules of warfare as he did and attacked him at night. The army of Bahraich suffered a crushing defeat as they weren't prepared for such treachery. It was a great setback for Suheldev. He was deserted. Yet, he didn't to lose the guts to fight. He sensed the dark and gloomy fate of India, if the Turks succeeded in their ambitions. He carried on his struggle against the Turks through guerilla tactics and prevented them from conquering Bahraich.

Prelude

Suheldev realised that no state could resist the collosal Turkish army all alone. They could only be overpowered through a unified retaliation. Hence,he declared a Dharmayuddha against the Turks and called for a decisive joint struggle to the Indian rulers. FInally, he was successful in forming an alliance of 21 kings ranging from the Rajputs to even the low-caste chiefs.

Masud's general Saifuddin was besieged at Bahraich by Suheldev which triggered Masud to arrive there with a larger force. But, little did he knew that he was moving fast towards his own doom. Masud who had not yet tasted defeat in his expedition now became suspicious of the allied force of Suheldev and the other kings of India.He hatched out a plan. He decided to place cows in the frontline of his army as a shield during the battle which would act as a pressure tactic making it difficult for Hindu warriors to attack them. But, Suheldev got knowledge of this vileness well in advance and freed the cows from Masud's clutches the night before the battle.

The battle of Bahraich

Finally, on 15 June,1034, the historic battle was fought at Bahraich between the Turks led by Salar Masud and the native Hindus led by king Suheldev. Suheldev strategically encircled and entrapped the whole Turkish army with his forces, making it impossible for any Turk to escape. Just like Lord Ram with his Vaanar Sena vanquished Raavan and his demonic army and the Pandavas vanquished the Kauravas in the Mahabharata, Masud along with his entire evil horde of 100,000 was laid to dust by Suheldev and his patriotic army. Masud was mortally wounded by Suheldev and succumbed to his injuries. Not a single Turk was spared alive in the battle, which shows the wrath and ferocity of the Indian warriors. The Turks had met the match for their malice and barbarism. The brutal extermination of the Turks sent a strong message to the foreign invaders, who didn't dare to set foot on India for another 150 years.

Suheldev's bravery, remarkable leadership and resourcefulness payed off and India got a long relief from such foreign invasions. The exemplary display of unity by the contemporary rulers is a testimony to the fact that no earthly power can conquer us if we Indians stand unified.

Legacy

Some call him a Jain, some call him a Rajput while some call him an Pasi. Yet, his great contribution in no way gets affected by his social background. A much needed hero in today's era, when India's integrity is threatened by outside as well as inside elements, who aim to harm the nation by direct and indirect means. Suheldev's saga motivates us to always take the initiative for righteousness and stand for the truth against all odds, regardless of howsoever, mighty and invincible the adversary is.

Still, it is a pity Salar Masud who did such harms to India is today revered as Ghazi Baba as he is rumoured overtime to be a great saint. A splendid dargah dedicated to him is located in Bahraich which is a place of worship for both Hindus and Muslims. On the other hand, Suheldev lies in obscurity.

Recently, the government has taken some initiatives to spread awareness about Maharaja Suheldev. In 2018, PM Narendra Modi released a commemorative stamp on Maharaja Suheldev. Various statues and temples have also been built in his memory. These measures are satisfactory yet not sufficient till King Suheldev gets a respectable place in our history books.

The problem with our written history

Suheldev's saga may seem to be a fantasy for some people, thanks to our modern Marxist historians who have purposely chopped out such significant incidents from our histiry books. Perhaps, we are the only nation in the world whose historians always underestimate their history and throw light on its losses and failures only. In spite of having such a glorious past, modern Indians have developed an inferiority complex as they are taught from an early age to look down upon their own history,culture and traditions and admire the West only. Not only Suheldev, but also the glorious tales of legendary kings like Lalitaditya Muktipida, Mihira Bhoja and Bappa Rawal who not only prevented India from Islamic invaders but also wreaked havoc in their own territories(the caliphate in Arabia and Central Asia) have also been ignored.

The inspiring sagas of the rulers, philosophers and all those who preserved and enriched our culture and civilisation needs to be brought to light so that these may be role models for the younger generation. The youth should be enlightened with the ideals and principles of our ancestors which enabled ancient India to become an economic, military and cultural superpower. Yet, it is equally essential to analyse and learn from the faults and failures of our ancestors as well so that are not repeated ever again in future.
